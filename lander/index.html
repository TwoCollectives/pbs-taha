#5: Uber

Driving for Uber is a flexible way to earn extra cash, especially if you enjoy driving. Expect to drive at least 40 hours a week to earn a decent amount.

Downside: Long hours on the road can be exhausting, and wear and tear on your car adds up. If you don't love driving or don't have a reliable vehicle, it's not a good fit.

#4: eBay

If you have an eye for bargains, reselling items on eBay can bring in serious cash. It might take 20-30 hours a week to earn a good amount, depending on what you're selling and how well you know your market.

Downside: The hustle requires you to be somewhat computer savvy. Plus, if you don't enjoy thrifting or managing inventory, this might not be the best option.

#3: Amazon KDP

Amazon Kindle Direct Publishing (KDP) lets you sell books and earn passive income. If you're a decent writer, it could take 15-25 hours a week to earn a good amount from multiple books.

Downside: Creating books takes time and skill. If you're not into writing, the learning curve may feel steep.

#2: Upwork

Whether you're a designer, actor, or web developer, freelancing can offer flexible work and high earnings potential. US freelancers can earn a good amount by working 15-35 hours a week on Upwork.

Downside: It can be unpredictable at first. Freelancing requires building a client base & reviews, and steady income may take time to establish.
